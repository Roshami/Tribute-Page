Timeline of Sir Isaac Newton

  • 1643
    • January 4: Isaac Newton is born in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England.
  • 1655
    • Attends The King's School in Grantham.
  • 1661
    • Enrolls at Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1665-1666
    • Plague Years: Returns to Woolsthorpe during the Great Plague and makes significant discoveries in calculus, optics, and the laws of motion.
  • 1667
    • Returns to Cambridge as a Fellow of Trinity College.
  • 1669
    • Appointed Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge.
  • 1672
    • Elected Fellow of the Royal Society.

  • 1727
    • March 20: Sir Isaac Newton dies in London and is buried in Westminster Abbey.

Key Achievements and Contributions

  • Calculus: Developed the mathematical field of calculus (simultaneously with Leibniz).
  • Laws of Motion: Formulated the three laws of motion.
  • Universal Gravitation: Discovered and formulated the law of universal gravitation.
  • Optics: Conducted experiments on light and color, proving that white light is composed of the spectrum of colors.
  • Reflecting Telescope: Invented the reflecting telescope, enhancing astronomical observations.
  • Royal Mint: Reformed the English currency, ensuring its stability and value.
